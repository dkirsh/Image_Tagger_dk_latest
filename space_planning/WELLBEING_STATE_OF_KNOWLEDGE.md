# Office Well-Being — State of Knowledge

*Space-planning workstream, `space_planning/`. Compiled 2026-07-15 (Fable/Claude Opus, from six parallel literature
searches). This is the companion to `STATE_OF_KNOWLEDGE.md`: where that synthesis covers what makes a floor plate
good for **cognition and configuration**, this one covers what makes it good for the **person-as-organism** — stress,
sleep, circadian health, movement, affect, and social connectedness. It is the evidence base behind the **well-being
(WB) strand** wired into `CRITERIA.md` (criteria C19–C24 and the WB re-reading of the SHARED criteria). Framed against
the keystone paper (Kirsh, *From Physical Code to Cognitive Code*, 2026-07-09): well-being is **not a rival code** — the
paper's "cognitive code" is umbrella, spanning "cognition, affect, physiology, and behaviour," and its dimensions 3
(thermal experience/pleasure), 6 (affordance/refuge), 7 (restoration/affect/aesthetics), and 8 (agency/the active body)
are already the well-being strand. This document lifts that strand out as its own scored objective with its own outcome
constructs and its own validation targets.*

**How to read the evidence tags.** **STRONG** = well-replicated, safe to build on. **CONTESTED** = real but with
debated effect size or boundary conditions. **PROMISING** = plausible, thinly evidenced; validate before trusting. A
fourth tag matters more here than in the cognitive synthesis: **promising-import** (the keystone paper's term) = a
finding that is bedrock *in its home discipline* but **scarcely tested in a real occupied building** — most of the
neuroaesthetics, awe, and much affect evidence is lab/image/VR, one-shot, on non-occupants, and does not yet license
claims about sustained mood or behaviour in a real office. Such criteria emit a *predicted hedonic/physiological
response*, never a *predicted productivity or well-being outcome*.

**The one-sentence summary.** The well-being evidence is strongest and most computable where it is **physiological and
geometric at once** — nature-view for stress recovery, prospect-with-refuge for felt safety, per-desk daylight/melanopic
for circadian health, layout-induced movement, acoustic/thermal load — and weakest, though not worthless, where it is
**affective and lab-bound** (neuroaesthetics, awe, colour); and the whole field is polluted with **inflated headline
numbers** whose own authors caution against them, so the over-claim watchlist (§5) is load-bearing, not a footnote.

## 1. Principles

Grouped by domain. Each: a one-line statement, the mechanism, key citation(s), an evidence tag, the **outcome construct**
it predicts, and its **computability** (geometry / image / spec-or-policy / outcome-only).

### W-A. Restoration and stress reduction

**A1. Nature-content views trigger fast psychophysiological stress recovery.** An unthreatening natural view
down-regulates sympathetic arousal within minutes via an evolved affective route (not attention-dependent). *Ulrich 1984;
Ulrich et al. 1991.* **STRONG** (direction) / CONTESTED (magnitude). Outcome: EDA↓, blood pressure↓, HRV↑, EMG↓, positive
affect↑. **Computable (image + geometry):** nature-content fraction in a seat's isovist / window solid angle — *the single
most operationalizable well-being variable* (→ C19, C9-content).

**A2. Restorative settings replenish directed attention (ART).** Environments high in being-away, soft fascination,
extent, and compatibility let fatigued directed attention recover. *Kaplan & Kaplan 1989; Kaplan 1995; Berman, Jonides &
Kaplan 2008.* **STRONG** (framework) / CONTESTED (factor separability). Outcome: sustained-attention performance, mental
fatigue. **Computable (partial):** soft fascination ≈ nature content (image); being-away ≈ enclosed retreat count (plan);
extent ≈ isovist depth (plan); compatibility = spec (→ C19).

**A3. Micro-restorative breaks and indoor greenery give short-term recovery.** Brief exposures to nature/greenery across
the day interrupt fatigue/stress accumulation; indoor plants show small positive stress/affect effects. *Lee et al. 2015;
Bringslimark, Hartig & Patil 2009; Largo-Wight et al. 2011.* **CONTESTED/PROMISING** (small effects, publication bias
flagged by the meta-analysts themselves). Outcome: perceived stress, attention lapses, EDA/EMG. **Computable (image +
partial plan):** greenery/vegetated-surface fraction; retreat/break-space count and proximity (→ C19, C23).

**A4. Prospect with refuge produces felt safety — the precondition for relaxation.** A seat with broad outlook *and* a
protected back releases threat-vigilance; unprotected exposure or entrapment keeps it engaged and blocks stress recovery.
*Appleton 1975/1996; Dosen & Ostwald 2016.* **STRONG** (seating preference) / PROMISING (stress-physiology link). Outcome:
perceived safety, vigilance↓, *and gates* cortisol/EDA recovery. **Computable (isovist — strong):** per-seat prospect =
forward isovist depth; refuge = rear/side enclosure & back-to-wall; entrapment = escape-route count & rear exposure (→
C11, C19).

**A5. Perceived crowding, not raw density, is the stressor; control moderates it.** Stress rises with density *appraised
as* crowding — loss of control over contact — and falls where occupants can withdraw. Low-partition open offices raise
measured stress/strain. *Stokols 1972; Evans & Johnson 2000; Evans & Stecker 2004.* **STRONG** (control moderation).
Outcome: perceived crowding, catecholamines/cortisol, strain, withdrawal. **Computable (partial):** density + visible
co-workers in isovist ÷ withdrawal-spaces per N; actual control = policy (→ C12, C17).

### W-B. Circadian light, sleep, and light-driven mood

**B1. Daytime melanopic light entrains the clock; the day–night *contrast* is the requirement.** High melanopic light by
day and low in the evening stabilizes circadian phase and alertness via ipRGC→SCN signalling. *Brown et al. 2022; Lucas et
al. 2014; CIE S 026:2018.* Mechanism **STRONG** / thresholds PROMISING (expert consensus, not RCT dose). Outcome:
entrainment, alertness, downstream sleep. **Computable (partial → needs spectral daylight sim):** geometry (distance-to-
window, orientation, window solid angle) *ranks* desks; a defensible melanopic-EDI number needs simulation. Targets (at
eye, vertical): **≥250 melanopic-EDI lux day, ≤10 evening, ≤1 sleep** (→ C10, C22).

**B2. Daytime daylight exposure lengthens and improves sleep.** Stronger daytime light → stronger circadian amplitude →
better-consolidated night sleep. *Boubekri et al. 2014, 2020; Figueiro et al. 2017.* **PROMISING** (consistent direction,
small samples). Outcome: sleep duration/quality (PSQI, actigraphy). **Computable (indirect):** geometry predicts *exposure
opportunity*, not behaviour (→ C22). ⚠ the famous "+46 min" is small-N, cross-sectional — direction only (§5).

**B3. Bright light improves mood; light therapy is an established antidepressant for SAD.** Higher daylight is associated
with better mood and faster depression recovery; therapeutic bright light rivals pharmacotherapy for seasonal, is
promising for non-seasonal, depression. *Beauchemin & Hays 1996; Benedetti et al. 2001; Golden et al. 2005; Lam et al.
2016.* SAD therapy **STRONG**; architectural daylight-mood **CONTESTED/PROMISING** (small, orientation-confounded).
Outcome: mood, depressive-symptom recovery, alertness. **Computable (partial):** east/morning-sun orientation + perimeter
access (→ C19, C22). ⚠ ambient office daylight ≠ 10,000-lux therapy (off ~40×) — supports mood, does not treat depression.

**B4. Chronic circadian disruption carries metabolic/health risk.** Sustained misalignment (mistimed or low-contrast
light) dysregulates glucose, insulin, blood pressure. *Scheer et al. 2009; IARC shift-work classifications.* Shift-work
link **STRONG**; extrapolation to ordinary office lighting **CONTESTED**. Outcome: cardiometabolic. **Not directly
computable** — a rationale weight on B1's day/evening contrast, not a scored metric. ⚠ do not port shift-work relative
risks onto daytime office occupants (§5).

**B5. Glare and flicker are well-being detractors — and glare destroys the daylight resource.** Discomfort glare (usually
from windows) causes eyestrain/headache and drives blind-closing, which then eliminates the circadian daylight; flicker
harms susceptible groups. *Wienold & Christoffersen 2006 (DGP); IEEE 1789-2015; IES LM-83 (ASE).* Glare **STRONG**;
flicker **STRONG** for susceptible, PROMISING general. Outcome: visual comfort, headache, *and indirectly* circadian dose
via the blind-closing feedback loop. **Computable (glare partial — needs facing direction; flicker = spec):** DGP per
seating orientation; **ASE ≤ 10%**; flicker is a luminaire spec (→ C10-glare, C24).

**B6. Light dose is a per-desk, time-varying *trajectory*; perimeter daylight is the distributed resource.** The same room
delivers radically different doses by seat, orientation, hour, and season; a desk's benefit is the time-integral of
vertical melanopic illuminance, and the core is the deficit zone. *IES LM-83 (sDA/ASE); Reinhart (daylight autonomy); WELL
v2.* Spatial backbone **STRONG**; melanopic-trajectory scoring PROMISING. Outcome: driver for B1–B4. **Computable
(spatial yes; melanopic needs spectral sim):** sDA300/50% **≥55% good / ≥75% excellent**; useful daylight ≈ 2–2.5× window
head-height depth (→ C10, C22). ⚠ **sDA (horizontal 300 lux) ≠ circadian compliance (vertical melanopic)** — different
plane, different quantity (§5).

### W-C. Air and thermal as health

**C1. Outdoor-air ventilation rate is the primary IAQ lever.** Adequate outdoor air dilutes CO₂, bioeffluents, VOCs, PM;
its dose–response to health/perception is among the best-replicated findings in the field. *ASHRAE 62.1; Seppänen, Fisk &
Mendell 1999.* **STRONG.** Outcome: SBS, perceived air quality, sick leave, cognition. **Computable (partial):** required
rate scales with plan area + occupant count; effectiveness depends on supply/return layout. Target **~8.5→ benefits up to
~10–25 L/s·person** (→ C18).

**C2. CO₂ is a ventilation proxy, not a direct toxicant.** Steady-state CO₂ indexes per-person outdoor-air rate; the
direct-cognitive-toxicity claim is unsettled. *Persily 2022; Fisk et al. 2019.* **STRONG** as proxy / CONTESTED as direct
cause. Outcome: ventilation adequacy → SBS. **Not geometry-computable** (sensor/operational); density from plan sets the
load. Target **<800–1000 ppm** (→ C18).

**C3. Low VOC and PM2.5 are required for a healthy interior.** VOCs → irritation/perceived-air-quality loss; PM2.5 →
cardiopulmonary/inflammatory effects. *WHO 2021; Wargocki et al. 2000; Sundell et al. 2011.* **STRONG** (PM2.5).
Outcome: SBS, long-term health. **Mostly spec (source control, MERV-13+ filtration);** geometry at the margin — intake
placement vs pollution sources, façade tightness (→ C18).

**C4. Ventilation/pollution load drives cognition — but the headline numbers are inflated.** Better ventilation and lower
pollutant load improve measured cognition. *Wargocki et al. 2000; Allen et al. 2016 (COGfx) + 2021 field follow-up.*
PROMISING; **the 61%/101% chamber figures are CONTESTED/over-claimed** — the real-building follow-up found modest
per-domain effects. Outcome: cognitive scores. **Not geometry-computable** (rides the ventilation levers). Use **ranges,
never the 61/101% headline** (§5) (→ C18).

**C5. Thermal comfort is a heat-balance (PMV) in conditioned space, an adaptive band in free-running space.** PMV predicts
comfort in mechanically conditioned sedentary settings; the adaptive model widens the band where occupants have operable
windows. *Fanger 1970; de Dear & Brager 1998/2002.* **STRONG** (each in its domain). Outcome: thermal comfort (PPD). Target
**PMV −0.5…+0.5, PPD<10%**; adaptive **T_comf ≈ 0.31·T_out + 17.8 °C, ±2.5–3.5 °C**. **Computable (applicability map):**
adaptive eligibility = operable-window reach / perimeter depth from the plan (→ C21).

**C6. Mean radiant temperature and radiant asymmetry are geometry-driven local discomfort.** Cold glazing / warm ceilings
create asymmetric radiant fields that cause local discomfort even at neutral air temperature. *ASHRAE 55.* **STRONG.**
Outcome: local thermal discomfort. **Computable (strong, geometry/façade):** MRT and asymmetry from glazing area,
orientation, occupant setback — *penalize desks placed <~1 m from large cold/hot façades* (→ C21). Limits: cool wall/window
<10 °C, warm ceiling <5 °C asymmetry.

**C7. Thermal pleasure (alliesthesia) needs non-uniformity — the neutral setpoint is not the optimum.** A thermal stimulus
is actively *pleasant* when it corrects a body load; spatial gradients and occupant-initiated transients generate pleasure
that PMV≈0-everywhere designs out. *Cabanac 1971; Parkinson, de Dear & Brager 2020.* **PROMISING** (strong physiology,
maturing design application). Outcome: thermal *pleasure* (positive affect, not mere non-dissatisfaction). **Computable
(meaningful):** solar patches, core-perimeter gradients, breeze access from orientation + fenestration (→ C21, C24).

**C8. Personal/adaptive thermal control raises satisfaction and widens tolerance.** Individual control restores agency and
enables alliesthesial correction. *Brager, Paliaga & de Dear 2004; Zhang & Arens (PCS).* **STRONG.** Outcome: thermal
satisfaction, wider acceptable range (~±2–3 °C). **Computable (partial):** operable-window/perimeter access = geometry;
fans/PCS = spec (→ C17, C21).

**C9. Thermal comfort is the top empirical dissatisfier, and its dominant failure is geometric zoning mismatch.** Across
large occupant databases thermal is consistently the lowest-rated IEQ dimension; the dominant cause is one control zone
spanning differently-loaded orientations. *Frontczak et al. 2012; Graham, Parkinson & Schiavon 2021 (CBE).* **STRONG.**
Outcome: overall thermal satisfaction (weight it high). **Computable (high value):** solar-gain-driven zone mismatch from
orientation + WWR + zone boundaries (→ C21).

### W-D. Physical activity and the active body

**D1. Prominent, visible stairs at the entrance (ahead of the elevator) raise stair use.** A stair seen first and closer
than the elevator flips the default from ride to climb; point-of-decision prompts add a modest, often transient nudge.
*NYC Active Design Guidelines 2010; Nicoll & Zimring 2009; Soler et al. 2010.* Signage **STRONG** (small); stair
design/visibility PROMISING. Outcome: stair use → incidental MET-minutes. **Computable (strongest geometry lever):** stair
in entrance isovist; `dist(entry,stair) ≤ dist(entry,elevator)`; stair-prominence ratio (→ C15).

**D2. Siting amenities for short walks converts routine needs into movement.** Distributing printers, coffee/water,
toilets, meeting rooms so they require a short walk accumulates NEAT and sitting-breaks. *Levine 2002/2005; Zimring et al.
2005; Hillier & Hanson 1984 (the analytic engine).* **PROMISING** (mechanism robust, RCT-thin). Outcome: step count,
sitting-break frequency, NEAT. **Computable (strong):** per-seat network distance to nearest amenity; distance
*distribution* (flag both extremes) (→ C15). Thresholds are design targets, not evidence-based cut-points.

**D3. Prolonged sitting is a modest, activity-attenuable cardiometabolic risk — the *rationale*, not a metric.** Total and
uninterrupted sitting is a risk marker via inactivity physiology; breaking it up helps. *Owen et al. 2010; Dunstan et al.
2012; Ekelund et al. 2016 (attenuation).* **STRONG** (risk direction) / CONTESTED (independence, dose). Outcome: sedentary
time, metabolic markers, mortality. **Not geometry-computable** — the warrant for D1/D2/D5/D6. ⚠ "sitting is the new
smoking" is rhetoric (HR ~1.1–1.5 vs smoking's 15–30×), banned from the code text (§5).

**D4. Sit-stand desks modestly reduce sitting and back discomfort; hard-health effects are small.** Height-adjustable
desks cut sitting ~30–120 min/day short-term (low-certainty evidence); multi-component (desk + policy) beats hardware
alone. *Shrestha et al. 2018 (Cochrane); Dunstan/Healy et al. 2018 (Stand Up Victoria).* **CONTESTED.** Outcome: sitting
time, musculoskeletal discomfort. **Not geometry-computable** — a furniture spec credit, ideally paired with policy. ⚠
vendor calorie/weight claims inflated (§5).

**D5. Short activity breaks acutely improve mood and energy.** Brief light walking raises vigor and reduces fatigue/tension
across the day — the acute affect benefit a movement-inducing layout delivers repeatedly. *Thayer 1987; Ekkekakis 2003;
Bergouignan et al. 2016.* **PROMISING→STRONG** (acute). Outcome: mood/affect (vigor, fatigue) — *the construct most
directly answering "well-being."* **Computable (indirect):** daylit-circulation %, walk-loop presence, break-space access
(→ C15, C19).

**D6. Walkable layout raises daily steps and light activity.** Longer, encouraged internal trips raise steps; the
steps→health relationship is strong even below the folk "10,000" target. *Zimring et al. 2005; Saint-Maurice et al. 2020;
Paluch et al. 2022.* Layout→steps PROMISING; steps→health STRONG. Outcome: step count, metabolic. **Computable (strong,
integrator of D1+D2+D5):** modeled layout-induced steps = trip-frequency (spec) × network distances (geometry); report as
*potential*, not realized. Health-relevant target **~7,000–8,000 steps/day, not 10,000** (§5) (→ C15).

### W-E. Affect, aesthetics, and awe

*Honesty banner: almost this entire subsection is lab/image/VR, one-shot, on non-occupants — reliable as *perceptual/
hedonic response to images*, **promising-import** as sustained mood or behaviour in a real occupied office. Outputs are
labelled "predicted hedonic response," never "predicted productivity/well-being."*

**E1. Curvature/contour is preferred and approach-eliciting; enclosure reads as threat.** Curvilinear interiors are judged
more beautiful and enter-able; sharp angularity carries an implicit threat signal; open > enclosed for approach. *Vartanian
et al. 2013; Bar & Neta 2006.* **STRONG (lab)** / promising-import. Outcome: aesthetic pleasure, approach. **Computable
(image):** contour curvature, angularity statistics, openness ratio (→ C24, C11).

**E2. Interior aesthetic response decomposes into coherence, fascination, hominess.** Three reliable perceptual dimensions
predict interior beauty; coherence and fascination are the strongest and the most image-computable, hominess is spec-like.
*Coburn et al. 2020.* **PROMISING.** Outcome: aesthetic pleasure. **Computable (partial):** coherence ≈ symmetry/regularity,
fascination ≈ entropy/spatial-frequency; hominess = spec (→ C24).

**E3. The "cathedral effect" — ceiling height primes processing style and a sense of freedom.** High ceilings prime
abstract/relational processing and freedom; low ceilings prime concrete/detail processing and confinement. *Meyers-Levy &
Zhu 2007.* **CONTESTED** (priming-replication concerns; real-building tests scarce) / promising-import. Outcome: processing
style, sense of freedom (mild positive-affect correlate). **Computable (geometry):** ceiling height, volume-per-occupant —
*allocate high-volume to creative/collaborative, low to focused detail work* (→ C24).

**E4. Vast/expansive volume can elicit awe, with downstream prosociality and reduced self-focus.** Perceived vastness +
schema-accommodation produces awe; awe shrinks the "small self," raises prosociality and generosity, and links to
well-being. *Keltner & Haidt 2003; Piff et al. 2015; Stellar et al. 2017; Rudd, Vohs & Aaker 2012.* Awe-as-emotion
**STRONG**; architectural delivery **promising-import**. Outcome: awe, prosociality, well-being. **Computable (trigger
geometry partial):** encode awe as a **compression→release contrast gradient** between adjacent spaces, not absolute size —
the *contrast* drives it (→ C24).

**E5. The Kaplan preference matrix (coherence/complexity/legibility/mystery) predicts interest and pleasure.** Moderate
complexity plus readability holds interest and sustains attention; complexity is an inverted-U (too much = overload).
*Kaplan & Kaplan 1989; Ulrich 1991 (positive distraction).* **STRONG** (scene preference) / CONTESTED (interiors).
Outcome: interest, aesthetic pleasure, felt safety (legibility). **Computable (largely, image):** complexity ≈ entropy/edge
density, coherence ≈ symmetry, mystery ≈ partial occlusion — *model the inverted-U, not a monotonic score* (→ C24).

**E6. Mid-range fractal/biophilic form is preferred and mildly restorative.** Curvilinear, organic, fractal-like form
(independent of literal greenery) is preferred and lowers physiological arousal, peaking at fractal dimension **D ≈
1.3–1.5**. *Taylor et al. 2011; Hagerhall et al. 2004; Joye 2007.* **PROMISING** / promising-import. Outcome: aesthetic
pleasure, stress reduction. **Computable (image):** box-counting fractal D, curvature stats — another inverted-U target (→
C19, C24).

**E7. Colour/warmth effects on affect are weak and context-dependent — compute the features, trust them little.** Hue
effects largely fail to replicate; brightness/saturation are more reliable than hue. *Elliot & Maier 2014; Palmer & Schloss
2010.* **CONTESTED (weak).** Outcome: valence/arousal (weakly). **Computable (features yes, mapping unreliable):** expose
warm-cool/saturation/colourfulness at *low weight, flagged* (→ C24, C19-mood).

### W-F. Social connectedness as a health variable

**F1. Social connection is a mortality-grade health variable — the warrant for treating connectedness as health.** Stronger
relationships predict substantially lower all-cause mortality; isolation, loneliness, and living alone are independent
risks via HPA/inflammatory pathways. *Holt-Lunstad et al. 2010 (OR≈1.5), 2015 (isolation OR≈1.29); Steptoe et al. 2013.*
**STRONG** (general/older populations). Outcome: health/mortality (upstream: loneliness). **Not geometry-computable** — the
warrant, not a metric. ⚠ **do not transfer smoking-equivalent mortality odds onto desk placement** — office loneliness is
milder, situational, reversible; use as direction/motivation only (§5).

**F2. Commensal space (shared eating/breaking) builds bonds.** Eating and breaking together generates repeated low-stakes
encounters that seed weak ties and trust. *Kniffin et al. 2015; Woolley & Fishbach 2019; Dunbar 2017.* **PROMISING.**
Outcome: social support, belonging, cohesion. **Computable (geometry):** communal-eating seats & area ÷ headcount;
centrality (mean walk-distance / integration from desks) (→ C23).

**F3. A personalizable home base supports belonging; hot-desking has a belonging cost (implementation-dependent).** Stable,
markable space is an identity/self-categorization channel; fully non-territorial arrangements can threaten belonging, but
team-neighborhood ABW can preserve it. *Elsbach 2003; Brunia & Hartjes-Gosselink 2009; Millward et al. 2007.*
**CONTESTED/PROMISING.** Outcome: belonging, identity, place attachment. **Computable (partial):** assigned-seat ratio,
team-anchor provision, contiguous team-cluster size (→ C16, C23).

**F4. Co-worker social support buffers job stress — a health pathway.** Available support moderates the health impact of
job demands and reduces stress reactivity. *Karasek & Theorell 1990; Cohen & Wills 1985; Uchino 2006.* **STRONG**
(support→health) / PROMISING (layout→support). Outcome: social support → stress/health. **Computable (partial):** team
co-presence proximity; availability of semi-private space for confiding conversation (not just open exposure) (→ C23).

**F5. Informal "third places" are social infrastructure — but require permission and a place to legitimately linger.**
Neutral, comfortable informal nodes host the casual contact that builds community; effective ones need proximity +
privacy + *permission*, not mere adjacency. *Oldenburg 1989; Fayard & Weeks 2007; Klinenberg 2018.* **PROMISING.** Outcome:
weak ties, belonging, loneliness↓. **Computable (partial):** informal-node provision/distribution per N, accessibility,
and enough pause-affordance (enclosure) to linger rather than a thoroughfare; permission = policy (→ C23).

**F6. Enable co-presence WITHOUT forced visibility — exposure ≠ connectedness.** Connectedness comes from *voluntary,
comfortable* encounter; permanent visibility/surveillance produces stress and withdrawal (the open-plan paradox), not
belonging. *Appleton 1975; Bernstein & Turban 2018 (~70% drop in face-to-face); Sailer & Penn (space syntax).* **STRONG**
(exposure ≠ interaction) / PROMISING (geometric optimum). Outcome: casual contact → belonging. **Computable (richest
target):** isolated-seat detection (low co-presence in isovist/path = loneliness-risk); co-presence opportunity per seat;
**every high-co-presence zone paired with a refuge check** — a maximally exposed, zero-refuge seat is a *negative* despite
high co-presence (→ C23, C11). ⚠ do not reward raw visual-integration maxima — cap/invert beyond a threshold, require
refuge (§5).

## 2. The outcome layer — what well-being is validated against (the L3 targets)

The WB criteria are hypotheses about human outcomes until validated against these measures (the keystone paper's methods
layer). No single measure is definitive; they operate on different timescales and axes and sometimes dissociate, so
well-being is a **multi-measure construct** — a geometry feature is never claimed to "lower stress" from one indicator.

| Measure | Indexes | Restorative direction | Timescale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Salivary cortisol | HPA-axis stress | ↓ / faster decline | minutes–tens of min |
| Heart-rate variability (HF, RMSSD) | parasympathetic/vagal tone | ↑ | seconds–minutes |
| Electrodermal activity (EDA) | sympathetic arousal | ↓ | ~1 s (catches glare/noise events) |
| Blood pressure | cardiovascular load | ↓ | minutes |
| Frontalis EMG | somatic tension | ↓ | seconds |
| Actigraphy / PSQI | sleep duration/quality | ↑ | nights–weeks |
| Step count / sedentary time | movement/NEAT | ↑ steps, ↓ sitting bouts | days |
| PANAS / mood scales | affect (vigor, fatigue, tension) | positive↑, negative↓ | momentary–weekly (ESM) |
| UCLA Loneliness / belonging items | connectedness | loneliness↓, belonging↑ | weeks |
| Cognitive tasks (PVT, n-back, serial recall) | attention/WM consequence | performance↑ | task |

## 3. Criteria mapping — the well-being reading of the rubric

The WB strand is scored through `CRITERIA.md`. New WB criteria: **C19** restoration/nature-contact (A1–A3, E6), **C20**
chronic-stress soundscape (distinct from speech-intelligibility; see the acoustic synthesis), **C21** thermal comfort &
zoning (C5–C9), **C22** sleep/circadian day–night contrast (B1–B6), **C23** social connectedness/belonging (F2–F6), **C24**
awe/spatial generosity & aesthetics (E1–E6). The WB re-reading of SHARED criteria: **C10** daylight → circadian health &
mood (heavy WB weight), **C11** prospect–refuge → felt safety (A4), **C12** crowding → stress (A5), **C15** active design →
movement/mood (D1–D6), **C16** territory → belonging (F3), **C17** control → the binding stressor (A5, C8). Computability
inherits from each principle: **strongest** where geometric + physiological (nature-view, prospect-refuge, daylight
backbone, movement levers, MRT/zoning); **weakest** where affective/lab (E-series, colour), which carry the promising-import
tag and low weight.

## 4. Tensions and contingencies

**Stimulation vs restoration.** Encounter, awe, buzz, and prospect (C1, C24, F6) energize; restoration, refuge, and quiet
(C19, C11, C7) replenish. The same person needs both at different times — so the floor must *provide both districts*, and a
seat cannot be scored well-being-good on one axis while failing the other; the fit-matrix minimum applies.

**Prospect vs refuge (the felt-safety knife-edge).** Prospect without refuge is exposure, which blocks the very stress
recovery the view was meant to give (A4, F6). Every prospect/co-presence reward must be paired with a refuge check; raw
visibility maxima are penalized, not rewarded.

**Thermal uniformity vs thermal pleasure.** The comfort standard (PMV≈0 everywhere) actively designs *out* the
non-uniformity that produces thermal pleasure (C7). The resolution: hold the *dissatisfaction floor* (no cold-glazing
discomfort, no zoning mismatch — C6, C9) while *permitting* gradients and occupant-initiated transients (solar patches,
breeze) that create pleasure — comfort as a floor, pleasure as the ceiling.

**Movement benefit vs friction.** Active design deliberately *lengthens* paths to induce walking (D1, D2, D6); taken too
far, distance becomes a complaint (and fights the collaboration criteria that *shorten* interdependent paths). Reconciled by
a target *band* of amenity distance, not a minimum, and by *whose* path and *to what*.

**Connectedness vs privacy, and connectedness vs collaboration.** Belonging needs voluntary comfortable contact, not forced
visibility (F6); and the health/belonging objective (any supportive weak tie) is distinct from the task-collaboration
objective (interdependent proximity, the Allen curve) — optimizing purely for collaboration can starve the diffuse
weak-tie network that carries the health benefit (F7-scope). Keep them separate objectives; do not double-count co-presence.

**Awe everywhere dilutes awe.** Spatial generosity is a *contrast* effect (compression→release); make everything generous
and the release has nothing to release from (E4). Awe is a selective, sequenced move, not a global maximization.

## 5. The over-claim watchlist (load-bearing — this field's inflated numbers)

The well-being literature is unusually full of memorable numbers that outrun their evidence; several are flagged by their
own authors. None of these enters the code as a coefficient without primary-source verification, and several must never
enter as coefficients at all:

1. **Ulrich 1984 "tree view → ~1 day shorter stay":** one small (N≈46) matched-pair, single-wing study. Foundational
   direction, *not* a dose.
2. **Nieuwenhuis 2014 "up to 15% productivity" from plants / biophilic "8–15% ROI":** small, condition-specific /
   advocacy-synthesis figures — the restoration-side analogue of the Fisk/Allen numbers. Not planning multipliers.
3. **Lee 2015 "40-second green micro-break":** memorable single-lab result; existence proof of short benefit, not a dose.
4. **Boubekri 2014 "+46 min sleep":** small-N (~49), cross-sectional, self-selected, wide CIs. Direction only.
5. **"250 melanopic-EDI" applied to horizontal desktop lux:** it is a *vertical-at-eye* value; misapplying it to the work
   plane overstates compliance ~2–3×. And **sDA (horizontal 300 lux) ≠ circadian (vertical melanopic)** — different plane,
   different quantity.
6. **Ambient office daylight ≡ light therapy:** off by ~40× in dose; daylight supports mood, it does not treat depression.
7. **Shift-work metabolic/cancer relative risks ported to normal offices:** mechanism plausible, magnitude unestablished.
8. **Allen COGfx 2016 "61% / 101% cognition":** chamber artifact (small N, extreme contrast, SMS scoring off low
   baselines). The real-building follow-up is modest. Use ranges, never the headline.
9. **"Sitting is the new smoking":** rhetoric — sedentary HRs ~1.1–1.5 vs smoking's 15–30×, and the risk attenuates with
   activity (Ekelund 2016). Barred from the code text.
10. **Standing-desk "burns major calories / reverses sitting damage":** Cochrane low-certainty, ~30–120 min/day sitting
    reduction, marginal energy cost; prolonged standing has its own downsides.
11. **"10,000 steps/day":** 1960s pedometer marketing; evidence-based benefit accrues from ~4,000 and plateaus ~7,500–8,000.
12. **Holt-Lunstad "isolation ≈ smoking/obesity" transferred to office design:** population epidemiology on older/general
    adults over years; do not attach smoking-equivalent mortality to a desk placement.
13. **Neuroaesthetics / cathedral-effect / colour as behavioural or productivity claims:** reliable as image/lab hedonic
    responses (promising-import for real occupancy); colour-affect is the weakest link and carries minimal weight.

## 6. Open questions

The dose–response of nearly every WB principle in a *real occupied office over time* is the pervasive unknown — the evidence
is overwhelmingly lab, cross-sectional, or self-report, which is precisely the gap the cognitive-code programme and its
experiment platform exist to close. Specifically: whether image/VR neuroaesthetic and awe effects survive habituation in a
lived building (E-series); the true magnitude of layout-*induced* steps and its metabolic payoff (D2/D6); whether office
daylight geometry moves sleep and mood at the doses architecture can deliver (B2/B3); the achievable fraction of the IAQ
economics rather than the chamber headline (C4); and the geometric optimum for co-presence that maximizes voluntary
connection without tipping into surveillance (F6). All are carried as explicit uncertainty; none is trusted as fact in the
optimizer until validated against the outcome layer (§2).

## 8. Verification boundary (RULE 0)

This synthesis is drawn from six parallel literature searches on 2026-07-15, each conducted from the models' training
knowledge rather than fresh full-text retrieval this session. The **direction of effect** and the **existence and
attribution** of the canonical works (Ulrich, Kaplan, Appleton, Stokols, Brown et al., de Dear & Brager, Holt-Lunstad,
Bernstein & Turban, Vartanian, Keltner & Haidt) are high-confidence. The **specific effect sizes and thresholds** — every
number in §5, plus Holt-Lunstad's odds ratios, the Cochrane sitting-reduction range, the Boubekri 46-minute figure, the
250-melanopic-EDI and sDA thresholds, DGP/ASE limits, and the ASHRAE ventilation/comfort numbers — are reproduced at
abstract/secondary level and **must be checked against primary sources before any is hard-coded as a coefficient or quoted
quantitatively in a published document.** The over-claim watchlist (§5) is the explicit record of which numbers are known
to be inflated by their own authors. Per the programme's discipline, **no WB criterion is trusted as fact in the optimizer
until it clears the validation gate** (`CRITERIA.md` §6) against the outcome layer (§2) — and the WB criteria, being
thinner-evidenced and more promising-import than the cognitive ones, must reach L3 (physiological/affective measurement)
before any well-being claim is published.
