# Cognitive + Wellness Code — Home Pilot & Collaboration Plan

Piloting the unified POE on campus, then bringing it to Zaha Hadid Architects

Working document · 21 July 2026 · method & collaboration (commercials held separately)
**How to read this.**A working plan for the method and the collaboration, not a contract. It sets out (1) the unified Cognitive + Wellness Code POE method, (2) a two-phase pilot on our own campus — a classical POE, then the same spaces redone with the unified code — (3) the instrument kit, venues, and team, (4) how the academic pilot and the ZHA collaboration relate as two clean tracks, and (5) the sequence for working with ZHA. Anything touching IP or human-subjects measurement needs professional/committee sign-off before it binds. Commercial terms are handled separately and are deliberately not in this document.
## 1. The idea, in one paragraph

Standard post-occupancy evaluation asks occupants to tick satisfaction boxes and checks a handful of numbers against code. It systematically misses the things that actually predict how a building affects the people in it. The **Cognitive + Wellness Code**is a single evidence-based standard that measures those deeper constructs across three **co-equal layers**— Cognition how the space helps or hurts attention, memory, wayfinding, and aesthetic response; Wellness the air, light, acoustic, thermal, and material conditions that drive health and long-run wellbeing; and Physiology the bodily stress and restoration signatures that self-report can't see — grounded in the research literature (the Knowledge Atlas) and, for design-time, read directly off renders by the Image Tagger. The plan below proves the method on ground we control before we put it in front of a client.
**Two tracks, kept clean.**The **academic track**(David's lab: the campus pilot, the instruments, human-subjects work, publications) validates and de-risks the method. The **collaboration track**(with ZHA) puts the validated method to work on real projects and post-occupancy evaluations. The academic pilot is what lets us bring ZHA a working kit, standard operating procedures, and a real before/after case study — not a promise. Keeping the two tracks distinct is also what protects the underlying system and keeps the university relationship clean (see §7).
## 2. The unified method — three co-equal layers

The method operationalises the **“38 things POE could measure but usually doesn't”**plus the materials/indoor-air appendix into a working instrument set. Condensed to the domains a pilot actually runs:

| Domain                  | What classical POE does                     | What the unified code measures instead                                                                                                                                                                                           | Layer                                                                                        |
|-------------------------|---------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Acoustic**| dBA spot-check; "is it noisy?" Likert       | Speech Transmission Index of irrelevant speech; speech-privacy (AI/SII); RT60 at multiple positions — paired with serial-recall/memory tasks                                                                                     | CW                                |
| **Luminous**| desk lux vs code; "have a view?" y/n        | Melanopic EDI at the eye (circadian light); CCT; Daylight Glare Probability (HDR fisheye); flicker/TLM; view *content* & restorative potential                                                                                   | CWP |
| **Thermal**| air temp in comfort band (PMV/PPD)          | adaptive opportunity (operable controls); mean radiant temp & asymmetry; thermal pleasure/alliesthesia; distal-proximal skin gradient                                                                                            | WP                               |
| **Air quality**| CO₂ as a ventilation proxy                  | actual air-change + cognitive testing; VOCs & PM₂.₅ (COGfx effects below code); olfactory quality; material off-gassing map (VOC/SVOC appendix)                                                                                  | WC                                |
| **Spatial / cognitive**| space-syntax integration; wayfinding Likert | prospect-refuge at each seat; cognitive-map accuracy (sketch-map, pointing); local legibility via eye-tracking; neuroaesthetic response to geometry — **and the Image Tagger reading all of this off the render at design time**| C                                                               |
| **Physiological**| comfort/stress Likert                       | salivary cortisol; heart-rate variability (HRV); electrodermal activity (EDA) — the stress/restoration signature self-report misses                                                                                              | P                                                              |
| **Behaviour & method**| one survey, once, weeks after move-in       | movement/seat-choice sensing; experience-sampling (ESM) over weeks; adaptive-preference & implicit measures; **task-stratified, building-and-occupant-fit**framing                                                              | CW                                |

Every row has a literature anchor in the Knowledge Atlas and, where it is a visual/spatial property, a computed proxy in the Image Tagger — so the same construct is measurable at *design time* (from a render) and *post-occupancy* (from the space), which is the whole point of a “code.”

## 3. The home pilot — classical first, then the same spaces redone

The pilot's design *is* its argument: run an honest classical POE, then re-evaluate the identical spaces with the unified code, and show — side by side — what the deeper method surfaces that the standard one declared fine. That contrast is simultaneously the methodology validation, the calibration data, and the demonstration case study.
Phase 0  
Classical POE
**What a normal POE would do**, done properly on each venue: CBE/BUS-style occupant survey (satisfaction, perceived noise/temp/air/light, "have a view?"), plus code spot-checks — dBA, desk lux, air temp, CO₂, occupant density. Output: the conventional verdict per space ("acceptable / not"). ~1 visit/venue.
Phase 1  
Unified code
**The same venues, the deeper instruments**(see §4): STI + speech privacy; m-EDI/CCT/DGP/flicker; MRT + adaptive audit; VOC/PM₂.₅/CO₂-decay + a material off-gassing walk-through; prospect-refuge + wayfinding + neuroaesthetic tasks; HRV/EDA (and cortisol on a subset); a short in-situ cognitive battery (PVT, n-back, Stroop); an ESM week; and the Image Tagger run on photographs/renders of each space. Output: a construct-level profile per space, and the **gap**vs the classical verdict.
Deliverable
A **before/after case study**("what standard POE said vs what the Cognitive+Wellness Code found") for 4–6 campus spaces, a validated **instrument kit + SOP**, calibration data that ties the Tagger's design-time proxies to measured outcomes, and (academically) a methods paper. This dossier is what ZHA sees.
## 4. Instrument kit (lean, borrow-first)

Built to keep the campus pilot lean. "Own" = buy into the kit; "Lab/borrow" = already in the lab or borrowable from campus facilities/other labs; assays are per-run consumables.

| Domain           | Instrument                                                                                                    | Source       | Operator                     |
|------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------|------------------------------|
| Acoustic         | Class-2 SLM + STIPA (e.g. NTi XL2 or equivalent); or a calibrated mic + impulse-response software             | Own / borrow | Stephan                      |
| Light — spectral | Spectral light meter giving m-EDI + CCT + lux (e.g. Sekonic C-800 class)                                      | Own          | Stephan                      |
| Light — glare    | DSLR/mirrorless + fisheye for HDR → evalglare (DGP); flicker via a low-cost TLM meter                         | Lab/borrow   | Tanishq                      |
| Thermal          | Globe thermometer + air/RH loggers (HOBO); skin-temp iButtons (distal/proximal)                               | Own / lab    | Stephan                      |
| Air quality      | NDIR CO₂ (Aranet4-class) ×2–3 for decay method; low-cost VOC+PM₂.₅ monitors; optional PID for VOC             | Own          | Tanishq                      |
| Physiology       | HRV chest straps (Polar H10) + EDA/GSR wearables (Empatica/Shimmer-class); salivary cortisol kits + lab assay | Lab / own    | Stephan (IRB)                |
| Cognitive tasks  | PVT / n-back / Stroop / SART on lab tablets (PsychoPy — already in Experiment_Maker)                          | Lab          | Tanishq builds, Stephan runs |
| Behaviour        | BLE/UWB beacons or seat/occupancy loggers; ESM via a phone prompt app                                         | Lab          | Tanishq                      |
| Software         | Image Tagger (renders/photos) · Knowledge Atlas (grounding) · Experiment Maker (preference elicitation)       | Ours         | David/Tanishq                |

Exact models to be chosen with Tanishq (he knows the hardware landscape); borrow-first wherever campus already has the instrument, so the kit stays a modest, mostly one-off outfitting rather than a standing cost.

## 5. Pilot venues — a spanning set on campus

Pick 4–6 spaces that (a) span the space types the Tagger cares about, and (b) give natural A/B contrasts so the method has something to discriminate. Suggested set:

| Venue                      | Type                          | Why it's in the set / contrast role                                                                              |
|----------------------------|-------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **The lab**| Research space we control     | Baseline & instrument shakedown; we can run repeated, controlled measures and the cognitive battery here.        |
| **Private faculty office**| Enclosed single-occupant      | High refuge / focus space; anchors the "good enclosure" end.                                                     |
| **Open-plan grad bullpen**| Shared open workspace         | The acoustic/speech-privacy and crowding story; likely the biggest classical-vs-unified gap.                     |
| **Two classrooms**| Seminar room + a lecture hall | Daylight/glare + STI + RT60 with the age-interaction; a naturally-lit vs a windowless room is a clean light A/B. |
| **A common/atrium space**| Circulation / social          | Prospect-refuge, wayfinding, restorative potential; the "signature space" analogue to a ZHA foyer.               |

Deliberately include one space we *expect* to score badly on a hidden channel (e.g. a windowless room that passes lux+CO₂ code but fails m-EDI and DGP, or an open-plan that passes dBA but fails STI). The whole demonstration rests on the unified code catching what the classical one waved through.

## 7. Two-track operating model & onboarding

The collaboration is between **David's group**(~3 people) and **ZHA**(a large practice; sponsors Patrik, Hannes, Christos). The clean structure:
#### Academic track — UCSD lab

- The campus pilot, instruments, human-subjects work, methods papers.
- Staffed by David + Stephan + Tanishq on lab/grant time.
- Produces the validated kit, SOPs, calibration, and case study.
- University keeps its academic freedom; no client-deliverable pressure on students/postdoc.
#### Collaboration track — with ZHA

- Puts the validated method to work: POEs of existing buildings and design-time checks on live projects.
- The underlying system stays David's and is *licensed* for use in architecture, never handed over — so it keeps improving for everyone who uses it.
- What ZHA makes *with* it in architecture is theirs; the engine and its growth stay with David.
- A small steering group (Patrik/Hannes/Christos + David) meets on a regular cadence; mutual NDA already signed.
**The one structural question to settle (needs university sign-off):**how, if at all, Stephan and Tanishq contribute to *ZHA project* work, given they are university staff. Cleanest options, in order: **(a)**they work only the *academic* pilot, and David alone carries the ZHA-facing work — simplest and cleanest; **(b)**a *sponsored-research agreement* between ZHA and the university brings their time in academically, with IP handled by the agreement. Keep university-paid time and client work from blurring together — it engages university IP policy and conflict-of-interest rules. Confirm with the university's tech-transfer / COI office before Stephan or Tanishq take on any ZHA-facing task.
### Onboarding — first two weeks of the collaboration

- **ZHA side:**name a single ZHA counterpart/champion; agree the one existing building for the first POE and the one signature entrance/space-planning target; provision access (renders/BIM for the Tagger, building access + facilities contact for the POE); confirm the data-protection basis for any occupant data (UK GDPR).
- **David's side:**deliver the pilot case study + kit + SOP; a half-day training for the ZHA design/research team on reading Cognitive+Wellness Code outputs; a short data-handling + consent pack for occupant measurement; a RACI for the first POE.
- **Both:**confirm the collaboration terms, governance cadence, and the IP boundary in writing before fieldwork.

## 8. Working with ZHA — the sequence

Given discussions are **live**, the move is to give them a concrete, low-risk on-ramp that shows the machine working:
Now
**Frame it in the current conversation:**"we're validating the unified method on our own campus first to de-risk it, and will bring you a before/after case study." Share the dossier + exec summary; the wellness-code update strengthens the health/IAQ argument for both codes.
Weeks 1–8
**Campus pilot**(Phases 0→1). Produces the showcase + kit + SOP + calibration.
First ZHA step
**One POE on one existing ZHA building**with the validated kit — the first joint deliverable, and the proof on *their* asset.
Then
**Embed design-time checks**— the Image Tagger inside the tools ZHA already uses — plus the one signature entrance/foyer and space-planning treatment; scale toward a certification standard.
