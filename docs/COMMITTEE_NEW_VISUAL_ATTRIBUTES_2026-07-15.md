## 2. The top 12 in full (construct · computation · evidence · skeptic verdict verbatim)

### V1 — `contour_angularity_index` (SHARED, Tier A, ceiling GREEN, rank 9.5)

**Construct.** Curvilinear vs angular contour character of the interior: sharp angular contours elicit threat-related response and lower liking; curvilinear interiors are judged more beautiful and elicit approach. The most-replicated form-affect finding proposed to the panel, converged on independently by four seats.

**Computation.** Tier A, deterministic OpenCV. Canny edges -> linked contour chains above minimum length -> Douglas-Peucker/turning-angle analysis with local circle fits. Decompose arclength into straight / smooth-arc / sharp-corner segments. Outputs: curve fraction (curved length / total contour length, 0-1) and corner density (sharp angles per unit contour length) as a separate threat subscore; optional eye-level-band weighting. Feeds the existing fluency-hedonics layer as a signed valence input. Base variant needs no segmentation; the person-masked variant rides segmentation and drops to AMBER.

**Evidence.** Bar & Neta 2006 (Psychological Science): curved objects preferred over matched sharp-angled ones; Bar & Neta 2007 (Neuropsychologia): sharp contours elevate amygdala activation (threat interpretation); Vartanian et al. 2013 (PNAS): curvilinear architectural interiors judged more beautiful, engage ACC, predict approach decisions

**Skeptic (VIABLE).** Computability holds: turning-angle/circle-fit decomposition of linked Canny contours is deterministic classical CV and genuinely measures contour shape, which nothing in the existing stack (edge sharpness = definition, fractal D = scaling complexity) carries. Real confounds the implementers must handle but which do not kill it: (a) barrel/lens distortion curves genuinely straight architectural lines — undistort or flag; (b) Canny fragmentation in cluttered photos biases toward short chains, so the minimum-length filter and corner-density denominator need care; (c) the measure conflates architectural contours with object contours (plants, fabric, people) — the person-mask variant honestly drops to AMBER. Evidence base is the real, multi-lab curvature-preference literature; GREEN for the base variant is defensible since it rides no pretrained model. One literature overclaim must be corrected before shipping (see citation concern), but it weakens one anchor, not the construct: Bar & Neta 2006/2007, Dazkir & Read 2012, and the Chuquichambi meta-analysis stand on their own.

**Citation concern.** Vartanian et al. 2013 (PNAS) is real and did find curvilinear interiors more likely judged beautiful with ACC engagement, but as I recall the paper contour did NOT significantly affect approach-avoidance (enter/exit) decisions, and the predicted amygdala response to rectilinear spaces was not observed. Selling it as 'predict approach decisions' is a misremembered overclaim — strip that clause. Note also Bar & Neta 2007's amygdala finding is for everyday objects, not interiors; fine as mechanism evidence if labeled as such.


### V2 — `spectral_discomfort_deviation` (WB, Tier A, ceiling GREEN, rank 9.2)

**Construct.** Visual discomfort / visual stress from deviation of the image's Fourier amplitude spectrum from natural 1/f statistics, especially excess mid-spatial-frequency energy (Wilkins pattern-glare line); slope also indexes naturalness of spatial statistics.

**Computation.** Tier A, pure numpy/FFT, sandbox-verified by the proposing seat (recovered slope -1.95 on synthetic 1/f^2 noise, target -2.0). Grayscale -> Hann window -> 2D FFT -> radially averaged power spectrum over >=4 octaves -> OLS log-log slope alpha (with R^2). Outputs: (a) naturalness = closeness of alpha to natural value; (b) discomfort deviation = CSF-weighted positive mid-band residual per Penacchio-Wilkins; (c) orientation-resolved stripe-peak detector for high-contrast periodic slats/louvers (from the Oliva-seat variant). Cycles/degree calibration rests on a DECLARED FOV/viewing-distance assumption emitted with the score.

**Evidence.** Field 1987 (JOSA A): natural scenes have ~1/f amplitude spectra; Fernandez & Wilkins 2008 (Perception): discomfort predicted by deviation from 1/f, excess energy at medium spatial frequencies; Juricevic, Land, Wilkins & Webster 2010 (Perception): discomfort tracks departure from natural image statistics

**Skeptic (VIABLE).** The strongest candidate in the set. Computation is trivially deterministic (windowed FFT, radial average, log-log OLS) and was sandbox-verified on synthetic 1/f^2 noise; the Penacchio & Wilkins 2015 CSF-weighted deviation is an actual published computational model being implemented, not an invented mapping. All five anchors are real and correctly characterized: Field 1987 (JOSA A) 1/f spectra; Fernandez & Wilkins 2008 and Juricevic et al. 2010 (both Perception) linking discomfort to departure from natural spectra with excess mid-SF energy; Graham & Field / Redies et al. on art sharing natural statistics. The declared FOV assumption for cycles/degree is exactly the right honesty move. Residual confound to document, not a kill: the spectrum measures the photograph, not only the room — defocus blur steepens the slope, JPEG compression and downsampling inject/remove high-SF energy, and noise flattens the tail; the implementation should restrict the fitted band away from the Nyquist end and emit an image-quality caveat. GREEN is fair: pure numpy, no pretrained models.


### V3 — `visible_vegetation_fraction` (WB, Tier A, ceiling AMBER, rank 9)

**Construct.** Visible live vegetation in the occupant's actual view (plants, green walls, foliage through openings) — the core Stress Recovery Theory / Attention Restoration predictor: unthreatening natural content drives parasympathetic recovery and directed-attention restoration.

**Computation.** Tier A, classical CV. (1) White-balance sanity check (gray-world deviation); ABSTAIN on grayscale/heavy cast. (2) HSV foliage-hue mask (green band, S/V thresholds) INTERSECT high local texture (Laplacian variance / local fractal-D above floor — excludes flat green paint and fabric). (3) Morphological cleanup. Outputs: pixel fraction 0-1, blob count, largest-blob share (one big plant vs distributed greenery), confidence flag; ABSTAIN when hue fires but texture test is ambiguous. Declared proxy: 'visible plausibly-living green material', not botanically verified.

**Evidence.** Ulrich 1984 (Science): tree-view patients recovered faster post-surgery than wall-view patients; Ulrich et al. 1991 (J. Environmental Psychology): faster physiological stress recovery during natural vs urban scene exposure; Nieuwenhuis, Knight, Postmes & Haslam 2014 (JEP: Applied): green offices ~15% productivity gain over lean offices in field experiments

**Skeptic (VIABLE).** Construct link is the best-evidenced in environmental psychology (Ulrich 1984 Science and Ulrich et al. 1991 JEP are real and correctly described; Nieuwenhuis et al. 2014 JEP:Applied green-office field experiments and the Bringslimark et al. 2009 review are real, and the heterogeneity is honestly converted into an uncertainty band). Computation is honest about being a proxy: HSV green-band intersected with local-texture floor, white-balance gate, ABSTAIN paths, and the explicit declaration 'plausibly-living green material, not botanically verified.' Known false positives — artificial plants (arguably still partially restorative per the biophilia literature, but unproven), botanical-print wallpaper/fabric (green + textured passes both tests), dark-green upholstery with weave texture — and false negatives (non-green foliage, flowers, autumn tones) are exactly why the claimed AMBER ceiling is correct; do not let anyone promote this to GREEN. Complementarity claim vs C19 is genuine: plan adjacency cannot see the desk-facing-blank-wall inversion.


### V4 — `mystery_occlusivity` (SHARED, Tier B, ceiling AMBER, rank 8.7)

**Construct.** Mystery — the promise of more information upon moving deeper (Kaplans' strongest preference predictor), formalized as Benedikt's isovist occlusivity: the fraction of the visible-field boundary that is an occluding edge to hidden, reachable space rather than a solid surface.

**Computation.** Tier B on the inferred plan grid. Cast 360 rays from the camera cell (optionally seat cells) -> isovist polygon; classify boundary segments SOLID vs OCCLUDING (radial jump discontinuity with walkable cells beyond). occlusivity = occluding length / isovist perimeter; flood-fill hidden-adjacent walkable area through occluding edges for mystery = occlusivity * log(1+hidden_area), room-normalized. Emit uncertainty flag when C3 intelligibility is low (mystery-danger decoupling); optional Tier-A depth-map corroborator (half-occluded deep floor regions, from the materials-seat variant). ABSTAIN below plan-inference confidence threshold.

**Evidence.** Kaplan & Kaplan 1989 (The Experience of Nature): mystery the most consistent positive predictor in the preference-matrix studies; Benedikt 1979 (Environment and Planning B): occlusivity — summed length of occluding radial isovist edges — as the formal measure of hidden adjacent space; Franz & Wiener 2008 (Environment and Planning B): isovist measures incl. occlusivity correlate with reported spatial experience indoors

**Skeptic (NEEDS_EXTRA_INPUT).** The literature is solid (Benedikt 1979 occlusivity, Kaplan & Kaplan 1989, Franz & Wiener 2008, Stamps 2004 meta-analytic heterogeneity, Herzog & Miller 1998 mystery-danger — all real, all correctly hedged) and the intelligibility gate is good design. But the computation as written contains a fabrication by the project's own standard: mystery = occlusivity * log(1+hidden_area) requires flood-filling 'hidden-adjacent walkable area' on a plan grid inferred from ONE photograph. Cells beyond an occluding edge are, by construction, unobserved by the photo-to-plan chain — the inference cannot honestly label them walkable, so hidden_area is either zero or invented. The magnitude term therefore measures the inference engine's willingness to hallucinate rooms, not the building. The occlusivity FRACTION alone survives: classifying isovist boundary segments as occluding vs solid via radial depth-jump discontinuities (with floor visibly continuing past the occluder along nearby rays) is computable from visible evidence at AMBER. Full Benedikt mystery needs a declared floor plan as an extra input.

**Fix required.** Ship two variants. (a) Photo-only, AMBER: raw occlusivity = occluding boundary length / isovist perimeter, where 'occluding' is certified only by visible evidence (radial depth discontinuity with walkable floor visible beyond the occluder on adjacent rays); drop the hidden-area multiplier entirely; keep the C3 intelligibility gate and Stamps uncertainty flag. (b) Declared-input variant: full occlusivity * log(1+hidden_area) computed only when an actual floor plan / occupancy grid is supplied; ABSTAIN otherwise. Never flood-fill cells the single-photo inference has not observed.


### V5 — `window_view_layer_content` (SHARED, Tier A, ceiling AMBER, rank 8.6)

**Construct.** Window view quality via view CONTENT — number of visible environmental layers (sky / landscape / ground) and vegetation presence in the view — a validated driver of emotion, restoration, and cognitive performance, distinct from daylight quantity or plan-level view access; codified in EN 17037.

**Computation.** Tier A. (1) Detect glazing: high-luminance quadrilateral regions on segmented wall planes (depth-discontinuity / sky-chroma cross-checks); rectify by homography. (2) If aperture interior is near-uniformly clipped (LDR blowout), window presence stays SCORED but content = UNKNOWN — designed-in abstention, never guessed. (3) Else deterministic layer rules: sky (upper, high Y, low texture, blue/neutral), vegetation (excess-green + mid texture, reusing V3's detector), ground/built (remainder). Outputs: layers_present in {0..3} mapped per Ko/EN 17037, greenery fraction, aperture solid-angle fraction. ABSTAIN if no window detected on any wall plane.

**Evidence.** Ko, Kent, Schiavon, Levitt & Betti 2022 (LEUKOS): view-quality framework — content (layer count), access, clarity; Markus 1967 (Building Science): views containing all three layers preferred over truncated views; Ko, Schiavon et al. 2020 (Building and Environment): window view improved emotion and working-memory performance vs no-view at matched conditions

**Skeptic (VIABLE).** Anchors check out: Ko, Kent, Schiavon, Levitt & Betti 2022 (LEUKOS) view-quality framework with content/access/clarity is real; Markus 1967 (Building Science) on layered-view preference is the standard historical anchor; Ko, Schiavon et al. 2020 (Building and Environment) chamber study did find emotion and working-memory benefits from a window view; Kaplan 2001 (E&B) and EN 17037's layer-based view scoring are correctly cited. The blown-out-aperture case handled as SCORED-window/UNKNOWN-content is the best abstention design in the set — that is the single most common failure of interior photography and it is never guessed. Correct AMBER: rides plane segmentation, homography rectification, and a heuristic window detector. Remaining failure modes to log, not fatal: mirrors, TVs, backlit artwork, and large luminaires can pass the bright-quad-on-wall test (the sky-chroma and depth-discontinuity cross-checks catch some but not all); curtains/blinds and night shots should route to ABSTAIN; a window onto an adjacent lightwell will honestly score low layers, which is arguably correct. Non-duplication claim is accurate — nothing existing scores view content.


### V6 — `subband_entropy_clutter` (SHARED, Tier A, ceiling GREEN, rank 8.5)

**Construct.** Visual clutter as encoding inefficiency: Shannon entropy of steerable-pyramid subband coefficients over luminance and chrominance — how incompressible the scene is per glance; the efficient-coding substrate of processing load and (dis)fluency.

**Computation.** Tier A, deterministic. CIELab; steerable pyramid (3 scales x 4 orientations) on L plus coarse chroma decompositions; per-subband fixed-bin coefficient histograms -> Shannon entropy; luminance-dominant weighted sum with weights taken from the published code, not invented. Output: bits + corpus-percentile 0-1, plus per-tile map for comparability with the legacy tiled proxy. Affect mapping inherits the existing inverted-U fluency machinery.

**Evidence.** Rosenholtz, Li & Nakano 2007 (Journal of Vision): Subband Entropy predicts clutter judgments and search performance; explicit efficient-coding account; Portilla & Simoncelli 2000 (IJCV): joint wavelet statistics as the currency of early visual texture encoding; Henderson, Chanceaux & Smith 2009 (Journal of Vision): clutter measures of this family predict search in real-world scenes

**Skeptic (VIABLE).** This is the field-standard clutter measure implemented as published, not an invention: Rosenholtz, Li & Nakano 2007 (Journal of Vision) really did validate Subband Entropy against clutter judgments and search performance; Portilla & Simoncelli 2000 and Henderson, Chanceaux & Smith 2009 are real and correctly used; the Reber et al. 2004 fluency bridge is honestly labeled proxy-grade and inherited from machinery already accepted. Steerable pyramid + fixed-bin coefficient entropy with the published luminance-dominant weights is deterministic; GREEN is defensible (no pretrained model). It survives the no-duplication rule only because it is explicitly declared a validation-grade replacement that reports agreement with the legacy tiled proxy — hold it to that: if the two disagree wildly on the corpus, the legacy proxy should be retired, not both shipped as independent signals (double-counting clutter in the hedonics layer would be a silent construct error). Minor confounds shared with all image statistics: resolution, JPEG compression, and depth-of-field blur shift entropy; the corpus-percentile normalization mitigates this only if the corpus is homogeneous in capture quality — state that assumption with the score.


### V7 — `feature_congestion_clutter` (COG, Tier A, ceiling GREEN, rank 8.5)

**Construct.** Visual clutter as local feature-space congestion: how 'full' the local distribution of color, contrast, and orientation already is — sets the salience threshold any sign, exit, or waving colleague must exceed to be noticed; directly predicts visual search cost.

**Computation.** Tier A, deterministic. CIELab; 3-level Gaussian pyramid; per level: color clutter = sqrt(det local covariance of (a*,b*)), contrast clutter = local std of band-passed L contrast, orientation clutter = ellipse volume of local oriented-energy covariance (4-orientation DoG bank). Minkowski pooling with published luminance-dominant weights, max over levels. Outputs: per-pixel clutter map + scalar, raw and corpus-percentile 0-1.

**Evidence.** Rosenholtz, Li, Mansfield & Jin 2005 (CHI): Feature Congestion as covariance-ellipsoid clutter, validated on subjective judgments; Rosenholtz, Li & Nakano 2007 (Journal of Vision): predicts search RT better than object counts; clutter as effective set-size surrogate; Henderson, Chanceaux & Smith 2009 (Journal of Vision): transfers to real-world scene photographs

**Skeptic (VIABLE).** COMPUTABILITY: the sketch is essentially the actual Rosenholtz Feature Congestion algorithm (Lab covariance ellipsoids for color, band-passed L contrast std, oriented-energy covariance, multiscale Minkowski pooling) — deterministic, well-specified, implementable in numpy/OpenCV without semantics. EVIDENCE: all four anchors are real and correctly characterized: Rosenholtz, Li, Mansfield & Jin 2005 (CHI) is the covariance/ellipsoid clutter paper; Rosenholtz, Li & Nakano 2007 (JoV, 'Measuring visual clutter') did show FC predicting search RT as an effective set-size surrogate; Henderson, Chanceaux & Smith 2009 (JoV) did test clutter measures on real-world scene photographs; Beck, Lohrenz & Trafton 2010 (JEP:Applied) is the local-clutter-around-targets finding that legitimately motivates the map output. This is the best-anchored candidate in the batch. TIER: GREEN is honest — no pretrained models, no depth. COLLISION: the declared 'sharper variant of the ad-hoc tiled clutter proxy' claim is credible because FC is behaviorally validated and locally normalized where the existing proxy is not; recommend retiring or cross-calibrating the old proxy to avoid two clutter numbers disagreeing. RESIDUAL CONFOUNDS to log in metadata, not fatal: sensor noise/high ISO inflates contrast clutter, and exposure/white balance shift the color covariance — mitigate with mild denoising and the stated corpus-percentile normalization. Also verify the exact pooling weights against the 2007 paper's published values (the candidate says 'luminance-dominant'; the published combination is contrast/color-heavy — check before hard-coding).


### V8 — `spectral_naturalness` (WB, Tier A, ceiling GREEN, rank 8.4)

**Construct.** Spatial-envelope naturalness via orientation isotropy: man-made scenes concentrate spectral energy at cardinal (0/90 deg) orientations, natural content is isotropic — the best-validated bottom-up statistic of perceived naturalness, upstream of restoration/preference effects.

**Computation.** Tier A, deterministic filtering. (a) Shannon entropy of the Gabor-energy orientation histogram (isotropy); (b) cardinal dominance ratio = energy(0/90 +/-15 deg)/total; (c) mid-band spectral shape; (d) optional weak green-hue co-signal with capped weight. Fixed weighted combination along the published discriminant direction, 0-1; also computable per-block yielding a naturalness map locating where natural content sits (window vs plants vs materials).

**Evidence.** Oliva & Torralba 2001 (IJCV): naturalness as the primary spatial-envelope axis, separable by orientation/spectral signatures; Torralba & Oliva 2003 (Network): natural vs man-made categories separable spectrally; Kardan et al. 2015 (Frontiers in Psychology): perceived naturalness and preference substantially predicted by low-level edge/orientation/color statistics

**Skeptic (VIABLE_CEILING_AMBER).** EVIDENCE anchors are all real and honestly staged (Oliva & Torralba 2001 IJCV spatial envelope; Torralba & Oliva 2003 Network spectral separability; Kardan et al. 2015 Frontiers showing low-level edge/orientation/color statistics predict perceived naturalness; Berman 2008/Ulrich 1984 correctly demoted to correlational downstream support). But two problems keep this off GREEN. (1) CONSTRUCT TRANSFER: the spectral naturalness axis was derived and validated on whole-scene databases spanning outdoor-natural to urban; using it as a WITHIN-interior gradient is an extrapolation the cited papers do not test. Isotropy is not nature: a cluttered pile of fabric, ornate wallpaper, or wicker furniture is orientation-isotropic; a minimalist plant-free room photographed head-on is maximally cardinal. The measure detects rectilinearity, and only proxies naturalness through it. (2) CAMERA-GEOMETRY CONFOUND: cardinal dominance is measured in image coordinates, so a rectilinear room shot in two-point perspective or with camera roll loses 0/90-degree energy without becoming one iota more natural — the number then measures photographic viewpoint, not the room. TIER: because the fix requires roll/vanishing-point rectification from the existing heuristic geometry stack, and because the interior-transfer of the validation is unproven, the honest ceiling is AMBER, not GREEN.

**Fix required.** Before computing cardinal dominance, estimate camera roll from the vertical vanishing point (already in the stack) and rotate the orientation histogram so 'cardinal' means gravity-aligned, not pixel-aligned; report the isotropy-entropy term (roll-invariant) and cardinal term separately rather than in one fused score; gate per-block scores on a minimum oriented-energy threshold so blank walls do not emit noise-driven entropy; keep the green-hue co-signal capped as stated and validate the fused score against human naturalness ratings on interior photos before promoting past AMBER.


### V9 — `fractal_mid_d_band_score` (SHARED, Tier A, ceiling GREEN, rank 8.3)

**Construct.** Fractal fluency: proximity of the scene's fractal dimension to the empirically preferred and stress-reducing mid-D band (~1.3-1.5), plus coverage of the visual field in that band — the response-curve scoring the existing D outputs currently lack.

**Computation.** Tier A, deterministic numpy on EXISTING intermediates (global box-count D and local-D tile field). (1) Fixed asymmetric trapezoid response kernel peaked on [1.3,1.5] with declared falloff constants; (2) band coverage = fraction of local-D tiles in [1.25,1.55]; (3) low-confidence flag when box-count fit R^2 < 0.98 (broken scaling; see V30). Output (band_score, coverage) with individual-differences caveat in metadata.

**Evidence.** Spehar, Clifford, Newell & Taylor 2003 (Computers & Graphics): preference peaks at D ~1.3-1.5 across fractal types; Hagerhall, Purcell & Taylor 2004 (J. Environmental Psychology): landscape-silhouette D predicts preference, peak ~1.3; Taylor 2006 (Leonardo) and Hagerhall et al. 2008 (Perception): mid-D viewing reduces physiological stress; frontal alpha maximal at mid-D

**Skeptic (VIABLE).** COMPUTABILITY: trivially yes — a fixed response kernel and a band-coverage fraction over already-computed global and local box-count D, pure numpy, with an honest R^2<0.98 broken-scaling abstention flag. EVIDENCE: Spehar, Clifford, Newell & Taylor 2003 (Computers & Graphics, 'Universal aesthetic of fractals') and Hagerhall, Purcell & Taylor 2004 (JEP, landscape silhouettes, peak ~1.3) are real and correctly cited; Hagerhall et al. 2008 (Perception, EEG alpha response to fractal patterns) is real; Abboushi et al. 2019 (fractal daylight patterns in real interiors) is real and is the key transfer citation; Bies et al. 2016 individual-differences caveat is real and to their credit. TWO CAVEATS that must ride in metadata: (a) the trapezoid falloff constants outside [1.3,1.5] are invented engineering, not literature — fine only because declared; (b) construct stretch: the preference/physiology studies used isolated fractal patterns and silhouettes, whereas box-count D of a whole interior photo's edge map mixes pattern D with scene composition — Abboushi 2019 narrows but does not close this gap. CITATION CONCERN (minor, flag not kill): the 'mid-D reduces physiological stress' claim traces to Taylor-group skin-conductance work that is reported in Taylor 2006 (Leonardo, a perspective piece) rather than a full primary paper — the physiological leg is thinner than the preference leg and the metadata should say 'preference: strong; stress-reduction: preliminary'. TIER: GREEN is fair since it rides existing GREEN image statistics.

**Citation concern.** Taylor 2006 (Leonardo) is a perspective/review; the skin-conductance stress-reduction result it reports was never published as a full primary study — do not sell the stress leg at the same grade as the Spehar/Hagerhall preference findings.


### V10 — `landmark_differentiation` (COG, Tier A, ceiling GREEN, rank 8)

**Construct.** Lynch's 'singularity': a salient element functions as a landmark only if unique among co-visible candidates — high salience with low uniqueness (four identical red columns) gives conspicuity without reference value.

**Computation.** Tier A, fully classical. Take the existing landmark-salience module's top-k (<=6) candidate regions; per candidate build deterministic descriptors (Lab histogram, LBP texture histogram, Hu moments, area); differentiation of candidate i = min over j!=i descriptor distance (chi-square + z-normalized moment distance); attribute = differentiation of the most salient candidate, 0-1, plus repeated-element flag when >=2 candidates nearly coincide (colonnade condition). ABSTAIN with <2 candidates. Scope declared: within-frame uniqueness only, not building-wide.

**Evidence.** Lynch 1960 (The Image of the City): landmark quality = singularity, contrast-with-context plus uniqueness; Sorrows & Hirtle 1999 (COSIT): visual salience of landmarks requires contrast AND distinctiveness; Raubal & Winter 2002 (GIScience): landmark suitability operationalized as measurable deviation of a candidate from its neighbors — the formal template followed here

**Skeptic (VIABLE).** COMPUTABILITY: yes — descriptor construction (Lab histogram, LBP, Hu moments, area) and min pairwise chi-square/z-distance over <=6 candidate regions is deterministic classical CV, and the ABSTAIN-below-2-candidates rule plus the repeated-element (colonnade) flag are exactly the right honesty moves. The construct logic is sound: appearance uniqueness among co-visible salient candidates is a defensible within-frame operationalization of Lynch's singularity. EVIDENCE: all three anchors are real and accurately used — Lynch 1960 defines landmark quality as singularity/contrast-with-context; Sorrows & Hirtle 1999 (COSIT) explicitly separate salience from distinctiveness; Raubal & Winter 2002 (GIScience) operationalize landmark suitability as measurable deviation of a candidate's attributes from its neighbors, which is precisely the template followed. The 'within-frame only, not building-wide' scoping is correct and prevents the classic overreach (a frame-unique red door may recur on every floor). TIER: GREEN holds ONLY IF the upstream landmark-salience module is itself classical; if that module rides plane segmentation or any frozen model, this inherits AMBER — state the dependency explicitly in metadata. Minor implementation notes: Hu-moment z-normalization needs a declared reference corpus, and differentiation should be reported alongside candidate count since min-distance over 2 candidates is noisier than over 6.


### V11 — `route_angular_continuity` (COG, Tier B, ceiling AMBER, rank 8)

**Construct.** Dalton's 'follow your nose' principle: junctions offering near-straight continuations are cognitively cheaper and preferentially chosen; cumulative angular deviation predicts wayfinding difficulty better than topological turn counts.

**Computation.** Tier B. Skeletonize walkable space to a centerline segment graph (medial-axis thinning); at each degree>=3 node compute minimum angular deviation from each incoming to any outgoing segment. Outputs: (1) fraction of decision nodes offering a continuation within 15 deg ('nose-followable junctions'); (2) mean cumulative angular deviation along entrance-to-deepest-cell shortest paths, length-normalized, inverted to [0,1]. ABSTAIN with <2 decision nodes.

**Evidence.** Conroy Dalton 2003 (Environment and Behavior): VR wayfinders select paths minimizing angular deviation and conserving linearity; Hillier & Iida 2005 (COSIT): least-angle segment measures predict pedestrian movement better than metric or topological distance; Turner 2001 (Space Syntax Symposium): angular segment-graph analysis formalism

**Skeptic (VIABLE_CEILING_AMBER).** EVIDENCE is the strongest part: Conroy Dalton 2003 (Environment and Behavior — the 'follow your nose' VR study showing wayfinders minimize angular deviation) is real; Hillier & Iida 2005 (least-angle segment analysis outpredicting metric/topological distance for movement) is real; Turner 2001 angular segment analysis is the correct formalism. COMPUTABILITY is where the adversarial pressure lands, on two counts. (1) SINGLE-PHOTO PLAN POVERTY: a Tier-B grid inferred from one interior photograph covers roughly one room within the camera frustum; a plan fragment with >=2 genuine degree->=3 decision nodes will be rare, so in practice this abstains on most inputs — acceptable (abstention is honest) but the proposal should say the expected abstention rate is high, and note the cited evidence is building/urban scale while the measurable fragment is room scale. (2) SKELETON FRAGILITY: medial-axis skeletons are notoriously sensitive to boundary noise, and a heuristically inferred occupancy grid has noisy boundaries by construction — spurious spurs manufacture fake degree-3 junctions whose angles are garbage, which would silently corrupt both outputs. This is fixable (see fix), so not a KILL. TIER: AMBER as claimed is exactly right, and the note that it is GREEN-grade on a real supplied plan is correct — recommend also registering it as a declared-input metric for real floor plans, where it will do most of its real work.

**Fix required.** Prune skeleton spurs shorter than k times the local corridor width (distance-transform value at the branch point) before counting decision nodes; require each surviving junction's branches to persist for a minimum metric length before measuring the continuation angle; additionally abstain when the walkable region touches the frustum/occlusion boundary near a junction (the 'junction' may be an artifact of the visibility clip, not the architecture); report node count with the score so downstream consumers can weight by support.


### V12 — `natural_material_fraction` (WB, Tier A, ceiling AMBER, rank 7.8)

**Construct.** Material Connection with Nature (biophilic Pattern 9): visible-surface fraction of natural materials (wood, stone), with wood coverage following a dose-response (inverted-U, ~45% optimum) relation to comfort and autonomic relaxation.

**Computation.** Tier A over segmented planes. WOOD = wood-band hue + oriented grain (structure-tensor coherence / Gabor anisotropy with single dominant orientation); STONE = low saturation, granular mid-frequency texture, no dominant orientation; reuse existing material-table labels where present. Outputs: wood_fraction, stone_fraction, natural_material_fraction (area-weighted, 0-1); wellbeing mapping via inverted-U centered ~0.45 wood coverage, mirroring the existing fluency machinery. Declared limit: cannot distinguish real wood from wood-print laminate — bounded stated proxy error since the cited responses were visually elicited.

**Evidence.** Tsunetsugu, Miyazaki & Sato 2007: 0/45/90% wood-coverage rooms — ~45% most comfortable; non-monotone autonomic responses; Nyrud & Bringslimark 2010 (review): interior wood associated with positive psychological responses, moderated by coverage; evidence base limited; Fell 2010 (UBC dissertation): wood office lowered skin-conductance sympathetic activation vs matched non-wood office

**Skeptic (VIABLE_CEILING_AMBER).** EVIDENCE: the anchors are real and — unusually for this literature — honestly graded: Tsunetsugu, Miyazaki & Sato 2007 is the genuine 0/45/90% wood-coverage room study (45% most comfortable, non-monotone autonomic response; note it is small-N, ~15 subjects, one culture — the caveat is carried); Nyrud & Bringslimark 2010 is a real review that itself flags the thin evidence base; Fell 2010 is a real UBC dissertation with the skin-conductance office result (dissertation-grade, never fully journal-published — keep that grade visible); Browning et al. 2014 correctly demoted to theory-grade. The dose-response SHAPE claim is legitimately anchored, which is rare. The move of defining the construct as VISUAL wood (so laminate false-positives are bounded proxy error, since the cited responses were visually elicited) is clever and honest. COMPUTABILITY is the weak leg and is why AMBER is the correct ceiling on two independent grounds: it rides plane segmentation (automatic AMBER per house rules), and the wood/stone classifier is genuinely fragile — brown painted walls, brick, cork, leather, and striped wallpaper share the wood hue band; striped or grained non-wood textiles pass the single-dominant-orientation grain test; stone-vs-concrete-vs-plaster are near-indistinguishable to saturation+granularity features. **Fix required.** Before shipping, run the classifier on a small labeled interior set (e.g., 100 hand-labeled planes) and publish per-class precision/recall in the metric metadata; emit a per-plane confidence and ABSTAIN on planes below threshold rather than force-labeling; require agreement between the hue/texture classifier and the existing acoustic material-table label where both exist, and flag disagreements instead of averaging; apply the 0.45-centered inverted-U only to wood_fraction (as cited), not to the combined natural_material_fraction, since the dose-response evidence is wood-specific.

## 3. What the skeptics KILLED, and why (kept as institutional memory)

- **V34 `maintenance_dilapidation_index`** () — The literature is real and the response shape is right, but the computation as sketched does not measure dilapidation — it measures 'things on walls.' The wall-plane module (illumination-model residual blobs = stains; high-frequency edge clusters on 'nominally smooth' planes = cracks) will fire on posters, whiteboards, art, signage, outlets, switches, vents, wall-washing light gradients, and hard shadows, all of which are low-frequency luminance deviations or edge clusters on wall planes. Distinguishing a water stain from a shadow or a framed print requires exactly the semantic judgment classical CV cannot deliver, and 'nominally smooth' presupposes material knowledge the pipeline does not have. In an ordinary occupied office photo, false positives would dominate the anomaly-area fraction, so the output would be a confident-looking dilapidation number that is fabricated with respect to its construct — the cardinal sin, and acknowledging the confound in the rationale does not remove it from the number. The one genuinely computable fragment is the suspended-ceiling module, where the regular grid supplies a strong structural prior and per-tile deviation (discolored/missing tiles) is a real, checkable signal.
- **V42 `egress_aperture_salience`** () — Fails on both computability and citation hygiene. Computability: 'door-shaped, taller-than-wide, low-interior-luminance, floor-connected opening' is a semantic category classical CV cannot reliably deliver — dark wardrobes, full-height curtains, mirrors, and black door panels all satisfy the rule, while the most important modern egress case, a floor-to-ceiling glass door to a terrace, is BRIGHT and gets excluded by the 'exclude high windows / low luminance = navigable' logic. That is the glass-house inversion by construction: the metric would systematically score the most escapable interiors as trap-like. Distinguishing a fixed floor-connected window from an operable glass door is not recoverable from one photo by any luminance/geometry rule. Evidence: the enclosure-to-exit-decision finding is Vartanian et al. 2015, Journal of Environmental Psychology (ceiling height and perceived enclosure); Vartanian et al. 2013 PNAS is the curvilinear-vs-rectilinear CONTOUR paper — right finding, wrong paper and venue, exactly the misattribution class the panel flags. Fisher & Nasar 1992 and Appleton 1975 are real and correctly graded. The honest home for this construct is the plan's declared door/opening layer — which the proposal itself admits collapses into V19's locomotive channel, leaving no residual value for the Tier-A variant.
- **V48 `hominess_composite`** () — This is the hue-affect situation wearing a factor-analytic costume, and the honest design there was abstention. The construct is real (Coburn et al. 2020's hominess factor exists), but the operationalization is invented at both levels: (1) no cited study provides a mapping from image features to hominess — unlike V43, where Wastiels supplies a regression fixing at least the weight signs across the exact predictor dimensions, here the choice of the three proxies AND their fusion weights are both fabricated, and the proposal admits it ('invented fusion weights', 'weakest computational grounding'). A number produced this way measures the author's intuition about hominess, not hominess. (2) Two of the three anchors are misused (see citation concern). (3) Computationally it stacks three noisy proxies (warm low-key luminance is exposure-confounded; 'fabric/grain texture energy' inherits every roughness-proxy failure from V43; soft-material fraction rides material segmentation) and then hides their individual error inside a weighted sum — the composite structure prevents the sensitivity honesty that saves V45. Fabrication is the cardinal sin; a composite whose loadings have no evidential source is a fabricated mapping with citations decorating it.
- **V56 `patina_wear_index`** () — Fails computability at the construct level: none of the three sub-scores discriminates temporal depth from ordinary photographic and material confounds. Low-frequency chroma variance fires on lighting gradients and colored light spill; dark low-saturation blobs are exactly what cast shadows and soiling look like; and matte-ness from a single photo is not recoverable — perceived gloss depends on light-source geometry relative to the camera, so a matte wall lit raking reads glossy and a lacquered floor with no highlight in frame reads matte. The index would score a shadowed corner of new drywall as 'patina' and a pristine varnished antique as 'new.' Distinguishing genuine weathering from factory-distressed finish or dirt is semantics classical CV cannot deliver. It also depends on 'regions V12 classifies as natural' — V12 is a sibling candidate, not a shipped attribute; if V12 dies or underperforms, this abstains everywhere. Finally, the payload is empty for the WB code: by design it abstains on valence, so the well-being layer receives a descriptive tag it can do nothing with; 'abstention-pattern study value' is a research note, not a reason to ship a metric. The hue-abstention precedent doesn't rescue it — hue is trivially and reliably measurable with only the affect mapping abstained; here the measurement itself is unreliable. Literature is real but correctly self-labeled theory-grade (Kellert's 'patina of time' is an attribute list, not an empirical effect), so there is no evidence anchor strong enough to justify the computability risk.
- **V52 `size_hierarchy_exponent`** () — Computability failure at the core of its own stated purpose. The metric claims to distinguish graded size hierarchy from mono-scale repetition, but perspective projection alone destroys that distinction: a row of identical chairs, windows, or ceiling tiles receding in depth produces a smooth gradient of image-plane component sizes, which the Clauset MLE will happily fit as an inverse-power distribution. The detector will report 'graded hierarchy ~ Salingaros coherence' for exactly the mono-scale minimalism it exists to flag. Depth-correcting areas would ride the depth net (still AMBER) and still fails under occlusion and slanted-plane foreshortening. Second, 'Canny -> closing -> connected components' does not yield countable design elements — edge maps chain unrelated structure together and fragment textured surfaces, so what gets counted is edge-ink blobs whose census is threshold- and exposure-dependent, not architectural components. Third, with a declared area floor and pixel-resolution ceiling you get at best ~1.5–2 decades of size range and often <100 elements; Clauset-Shalizi-Newman fitting is famously unreliable in that regime, so the KS gate will mostly return UNKNOWN, and the metric's yield collapses. Construct evidence is honestly labeled theory-grade (Salingaros & West 1999 is real but illustrative, not empirical validation of a wellbeing/cognition outcome), so there is no evidence weight to justify carrying these computability defects.
- **V54 `facade_pareidolia_salience`** () — The mechanism citations are real and the scoping (salience only, Sussman & Hollander flagged as non-peer-reviewed) is commendably honest — but honesty about the downstream link does not rescue the measurement itself, and that is where this dies. The pareidolia literature (Taubert 2017; Liu 2014; Palmer & Clifford 2020) used stimuli that HUMANS verified as face-like; nothing anywhere validates that a Haar frontal-face cascade's hits on architecture, or a hand-crafted 'two co-elevated dark components plus a third below' template, correspond to what humans experience as pareidolic. Haar cascades are tuned to photographic face statistics and on non-face imagery produce sparse, essentially random texture hits; the geometric test detects a superset (any symmetric triadic dark arrangement) most members of which trigger no face percept, and it substantially overlaps the existing horizontal-symmetry attribute at local scale. So the number produced is 'symmetric triadic configuration density', not 'pareidolia salience' — an unvalidated detector-construct gap that is precisely the class of quiet fabrication this panel exists to catch. There is also a domain mismatch: the construct is facade-centric while the pipeline scores single interior photographs, so yield would be near zero even if the detector worked. Making it defensible requires a human-rating validation study of the detector on architectural imagery, which is a research project, not a computation fix.
